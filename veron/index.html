Veron — Men’s Wear E-commerce App

  • Homepage

E-commerce Mobile UI Conversion Case Study

Overview

Veron is a premium men’s wear shopping app concept focused on fast discovery, clean hierarchy, and a confident brand feel. The goal was to build a browsing and product discovery experience that stays elegant—even with filters, categories, and dense product lists.

My role: End-to-end product design (UX flows, UI system, high-fidelity screens, interaction states).

Timeline: 1–2 weeks

Platform: Mobile (iOS/Android)

Deliverables: Flow, UI kit, key screens

Tools: Figma

The problem

Men’s fashion shopping requires speed and confidence. Users need to scan product grids quickly, narrow options with filters, and trust the brand—without the interface feeling cluttered or “discount-like.”

Goals

  • Make browsing and scanning fast (clear hierarchy, clean grids)
  • Reduce friction in filtering and searching
  • Maintain a premium brand feel through typography and spacing
  • Create reusable components to scale categories and collections

Process

1) UX flow

Defined the core path: onboarding → home → category → PLP → PDP → cart.

2) Component system

Built reusable patterns for cards, chips, filters, headers, and states.

3) High-fidelity UI

Applied typography-driven hierarchy and spacing rules to keep density readable.

Key screens

Replace the image paths with your exports. If you don’t have multiple screens yet, keep one image and we’ll build the story around it.

Outcome

A refined, conversion-friendly mobile shopping experience with clear discovery, consistent components, and a premium brand tone. The system is designed to scale into more categories, promotions, and personalization without redesigning the foundation.

What I’d improve next

  • Add usability testing around filters and product comparison
  • Introduce wishlists and “recently viewed” for retention
  • Design checkout and payment states (errors, loading, recovery)
