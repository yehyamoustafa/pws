Veron — Men’s Wear E-commerce App

The brief: Veron needed a premium mobile shopping experience for men’s wear—covering onboarding, browsing, and product discovery with a clean, luxury feel.

The challenge: Apparel shopping lives or dies by speed and clarity: users need to find items fast, filter confidently, and trust the brand—without the UI feeling cluttered or “discount-store.”

My approach: I designed a streamlined flow from sign-in to collection browsing, emphasizing typography-driven hierarchy, scannable product grids, and frictionless filters/search—keeping components consistent and scalable for future categories.

The result: A refined, conversion-friendly e-commerce app concept that supports fast discovery, polished brand perception, and a clear path from browsing to purchase.

The Rig — Internal Document Operations Dashboard

The brief: The internal team asked for a single dashboard to manage document intake, approvals, and audit reporting without jumping between tools.

The challenge: Workflows were scattered across folders, manual trackers, and back-and-forth messages, making it hard to find the latest file, track ownership, or prove compliance when needed.

My approach: I led information architecture and a reusable UI system—structured navigation, data tables, and action patterns designed to stay readable even in a dense, ops-heavy environment.

The result: A centralized operations dashboard that streamlines approvals, improves visibility, and keeps audit-ready tracking clean, fast, and reliable.

Numbase — Corporate Website & Messaging Refresh

Numbase × Mastercard — Mobile Onboarding Flow

The brief: The client asked for a smoother signup flow that keeps trust high while guiding users through verification steps required for a fintech product.

The challenge: Multi-step onboarding can lose users fast—especially when verification feels confusing, slow, or “uncertain” (users don’t know what’s next or why).

My approach: I designed an end-to-end onboarding journey with clear step progression, stronger trust cues, and tighter UX writing—while accounting for edge cases like OTP resend, incorrect inputs, and recovery states.

The result: A clean, confidence-building onboarding experience that reduces friction and makes compliance-heavy steps feel simple and guided.

KODEQ — Brand Identity for a Marketing Product Team

The brief: The team needed a modern identity that works across product UI, marketing assets, and launch materials—without feeling inconsistent from channel to channel.

The challenge: Without a defined system, teams tend to improvise. That leads to inconsistent logo usage, mismatched typography, and messy visuals during launches and product updates.

My approach: I built a scalable wordmark and practical brand rules—clear spacing, typography, and usage guidance designed for real-world speed (marketing + product teams shipping fast).

The result: A cohesive brand identity system that stays consistent across UI and marketing, helping launches look “ready-to-ship” instead of stitched together.

KODEQ — Responsive Website & Service Discovery

The brief: The goal was to help visitors understand services quickly and guide them toward action—especially on mobile, where attention drops fast.

DS2 — Rebrand for Clarity, Consistency, and Scale

The brief: DS2 needed a rebrand that improves legibility and consistency across print, digital touchpoints, and product UI—especially at small sizes.

My approach: I simplified the mark into a cleaner, geometric form and defined usage rules—spacing, contrast, and sizing—to ensure the identity holds up across channels.

Musikata — Mobile Player Concept with Personalization

The brief: The concept explored a modern mobile listening experience that feels personal, fast, and clean—without overwhelming users with controls.

The challenge: Music apps must balance discovery, playback control, and queue behavior. When these compete, the UI becomes cluttered and users lose flow.

My approach: I designed a clear navigation model and scalable components for playback, queue, and browsing—treating each as a reusable module so the product can grow without redesigning core screens.

The result: An immersive, dark-first player concept built for clarity and extensibility—ready to support personalization and future feature expansion.
